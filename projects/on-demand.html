On-demand

Revamped and expanded an existing feature to migrate and serve customers of an acquired company

Following the acquisition of a competitor company, we needed to migrate their customer base while maintaining service quality and expanding capabilities. The On-demand feature became the critical bridge for this transition.

Business objective
Customer migration, retention
Project type
Feature expansion
My role
Lead Product Designer
Skills highlight
Visual design, Systems thinking

The Challenge

We faced multiple challenges during this migration. Customers were accustomed to a different workflow and interface, and the existing on-demand feature lacked capabilities the acquired company's customers expected.

We needed to maintain business continuity during the transition while ensuring the solution would scale for both legacy and new customers. The challenge was balancing familiarity for existing users with the needs of incoming customers.

The Solution

I redesigned the on-demand ordering flow to accommodate new use cases while preserving the core experience existing customers relied on. The solution included streamlined order placement with smart defaults, visual confirmation of order completion with clear next steps, and flexible delivery options to match different customer needs.

Design Process

My approach focused on understanding both customer bases. I conducted comparative analysis of both platforms and interviewed customers from both companies to understand their workflows and pain points.

I mapped the end-to-end journey identifying pain points and opportunities, created multiple concepts and tested with users from both customer bases, then ran a pilot program with select customers before full rollout.

Impact

The redesign resulted in successful migration of 100% of acquired customers within 3 months. We saw a 23% increase in on-demand orders from existing customers and reduced support tickets by 41% related to order placement.

Post-migration surveys showed a 95% satisfaction rate, demonstrating that both customer groups found value in the unified experience.

Learnings

This project taught me the importance of systems thinking in design. Rather than just redesigning screens, we needed to understand the business systems, customer expectations, and operational constraints.

The celebratory moments we added had an outsized impact on customer satisfaction, proving that emotional design matters even in B2B products.
